Wires, Cables, and Wifi

The Internet is a system designed to move information from one place to another. It is a way to send information made of bits which are sets of 1’s and 0’s. This is known as binary code. 8 bits together is 1 byte, 1000 bytes is 1 kilo byte, and 1000 kilo bytes is 1 mega byte (a typical song is about 3-4 mega bytes). No matter what you are sending, everything on the Internet is sent around as bits. Bits can be sent in three different ways- with electricity, light, or radio waves.

Using electricity, bits are sent per a certain amount of time. The speed at which the bits are sent are a result of something called bandwidth. Bandwidth is transmission capacity, measured by bitrate. Bitrate is the number of bits per second a system can transmit. This can also be known as latency, which is the time it takes for a bit to travel from a sender to a receiver. The major benefit of using electricity is that it is relatively cheap but its downside is there is a lot of signal loss.

Light takes advantage of fiber optic cables. These cables, on the inside, are coated with a reflective surface that allows the light to travel through without any interruption. Fiber optic cables are so reliable they can travel across oceans. They are also one of the fastest, if not the fastest, way to send information from one place to another. The only downside is that that are super expensive to install and set up, and they are not the easiest to work with.

Sending information via radio uses radio signals. The machines that transmit the signal have to change the 1’s and 0’s to radio wave frequencies. Then, the receiving machines have to change those radio frequencies back to the binary code. Not having any wires can be a great thing, it is one less thing you have to manage. However, it comes with signal lass. Wireless connections can only reach so far and if you step out of range, you will lose service.
